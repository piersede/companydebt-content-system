# Component Pack: Enforcement Pages

Apply 2–3 of the following. Never all. Full specs: `editorial-os/28-readability-components.md`.

## Recommended stack

**1. Risk Warning** (`.cd-callout--risk-warning`) — Primary
- Place in the top third. Enforcement pages exist because a deadline or action is imminent.
- Evidence level A only. Cite the specific statutory power and the consequence of inaction.

**2. Timeline Reality** (`.cd-callout--timeline-reality`) — Primary
- Place where the key deadline or procedural timeline appears.
- Do not place adjacent to Risk Warning. At least one prose section between them.

**3. Process Steps** (`.cd-process-steps`) — Primary
- Use to map the enforcement procedure step by step.
- 3–5 steps. Keeps the process legible for a reader under pressure.

**Creditor Perspective** (`.cd-callout--creditor-perspective`) — Secondary
- Place where the page explains why the creditor is acting and what they are trying to achieve.
- Helps the reader understand the creditor's incentive structure.

## Rhythm constraint

Max 3 components. Risk Warning must be in the top third. Timeline Reality not adjacent to Risk Warning. All Level A callouts require evidence citation.
