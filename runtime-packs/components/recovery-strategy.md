# Component Pack: Recovery Strategy Pages

Apply 2–3 of the following. Never all. Full specs: `editorial-os/28-readability-components.md`.

## Recommended stack

**1. Recovery Path** (`.cd-callout--recovery-path`) — Primary
- Place after the options section.
- Why: Recovery strategy pages are about mapping the route forward. The Recovery Path box frames the decision fork explicitly.

**2. Key Takeaway** (`.cd-key-takeaway`) — Primary
- Place after the most complex section (often the creditor negotiation or restructuring mechanics section).
- 1–3 sentences. Consolidates the main decision for a reader scanning the page.

**3. Orientation Block** (`.cd-orientation-block`) — Primary
- Place near the top if the page serves directors at different stages (distressed vs. contingency planning).
- Skip if the opening paragraphs already identify the audience clearly.

**Inline CTA** (`.cd-inline-cta`) — Secondary
- Place after the highest-intent section (typically after the options or first steps section).
- One per page. Not within two sections of the page-end CTA.

## Rhythm constraint

Max 3 components. Recovery Path and Key Takeaway must not be adjacent. Inline CTA must be well separated from any other CTA element.
