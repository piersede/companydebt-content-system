If you are a director of a company in financial difficulty, insolvency advice is not a luxury. It is the single most important step you can take to protect yourself personally and to manage the company's closure or rescue properly.

We work with directors seeking insolvency advice every day. The question we hear most often is not "what are my options?" — it is "why did I not call sooner?" Every director who waited wishes they had not. Every director who called early had more options, lower costs, and a better conduct position than those who delayed. The advice itself is free (most IPs offer free initial consultations), confidential, and does not commit you to anything. The cost of not having the conversation is measured in months of additional trading losses, increased personal exposure, and options that closed while you were deciding whether to pick up the phone.

Quick Answer: Why Insolvency Advice Matters for Directors

A licensed insolvency practitioner assesses your company's financial position, determines whether it is solvent or insolvent, and explains all available options: informal negotiation, HMRC Time to Pay, CVA, administration, MVL, CVL, or dissolution. They also assess your personal exposure: personal guarantees, overdrawn loan accounts, wrongful trading risk, and potential disqualification.

The date you first sought professional advice is one of the most important data points in the liquidator's conduct report. Section 214(3) of the Insolvency Act provides a defence to wrongful trading if you took every step a reasonably diligent person would take to minimise creditor losses. Seeking insolvency advice is the clearest demonstration of that diligence.

When You Should Seek Insolvency Advice

Before you miss a payment. Before HMRC serves a statutory demand. Before a supplier instructs solicitors. We find that directors who seek advice at these trigger points have significantly better outcomes:

  • You are choosing which creditors to pay each month. This is evidence of cash-flow insolvency. Seek advice now.
  • HMRC debts are accumulating. VAT, PAYE, or Corporation Tax arrears compound quickly. HMRC is the most common petitioning creditor.
  • A creditor has served a statutory demand or filed a winding-up petition. You have days, not months, to act.
  • You are funding the business from personal savings. The business cannot sustain itself.
  • Your accountant has raised concerns. The liquidator will ask what your accountant told you, and when.

We tell every director: if you recognise any of these, the time for advice was last week. The second-best time is today.

What Happens in an Insolvency Advice Consultation

The initial consultation takes 30-60 minutes. The practitioner will:

  • Review your company's financial position — what you owe, who you owe it to, what cash is available
  • Apply the statutory insolvency tests (cash-flow and balance-sheet) to determine whether the company is insolvent
  • Explain every available option — from informal negotiation to formal liquidation
  • Assess your personal exposure — guarantees, loan accounts, wrongful trading risk
  • Give you a realistic view of what each option costs, how long it takes, and what the likely outcome is

We emphasise: the practitioner is not there to push you into liquidation. In roughly a third of consultations, the company does not need to be liquidated. It needs a different route that the director did not know existed.

How Insolvency Advice Protects You Personally

The conduct report the liquidator files with the Insolvency Service includes the date you first sought professional advice. A report that says "the director consulted a licensed IP on 15 January and entered a CVL on 2 February" reads very differently from one that says "the director did not seek professional advice at any point and the company continued to trade for a further eight months before a creditor petitioned."

We have seen this distinction determine the difference between a clean conduct report and a disqualification proceeding. The advice costs nothing. The protection it provides costs nothing. The failure to seek it costs everything.

FAQs About Insolvency Advice for Directors

Is insolvency advice really free?

The initial consultation is free. If you proceed to a formal process, that process has fees. But the consultation that determines which route you need costs nothing.

Will seeking advice mean my company is liquidated?

No. The consultation is diagnostic. Many consultations result in recommendations for informal negotiation, TTP, or CVA — not liquidation. You decide what to do with the advice.

How does seeking advice protect me from wrongful trading?

Section 214(3) of the Insolvency Act provides a defence if you took every step a reasonably diligent person would take to minimise creditor losses. Seeking professional insolvency advice and acting on it is the strongest evidence of that diligence. The date you sought advice is recorded in the liquidator's conduct report.
