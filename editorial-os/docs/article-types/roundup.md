# Roundup Article Type — Authorship and Structure Rules

Extends the base rules in `docs/human-authorship-voice-engine.md` with roundup-specific requirements.

## Roundup-specific authorship rules

- No ghost listicles: every option in the list must receive substantive, specific, use-case-grounded treatment
- "Best for" labels must be justified with actual workflow or buyer-fit reasoning, not just repeated as a category tag
- Do not use generic baseline pros as differentiators (e.g., "easy to use", "good support" without specifics)
- Do not compress non-winning entries into filler; every entry deserves genuine analysis
- Every top recommendation must still acknowledge a real trade-off (Rule E)
- Avoid generic listicle intro patterns ("Looking for the best X? Here are our top picks...")
- Avoid neutral, over-balanced roundup conclusions that fail to compress the decision (Rule J)

## Roundup structure requirements

- Decision utility in the first fold: the reader must know the top answer within the first screen
- Each entry must include: best for, weak for, workflow mechanics, pricing model, practice-size fit
- At least one insight block that synthesises patterns across the options rather than summarising individual tools
- Conclusion must map reader situations to specific recommendations, not restate the list

## Roundup pronoun defaults

Primary: we
Secondary: you
Tone: authoritative, specific, decision-oriented
Human signal: visible evaluation methodology, compressed verdicts per tool, honest about trade-offs

## Roundup-specific fail conditions

- **FAIL** if any entry is a ghost (feature summary without substantive evaluation)
- **FAIL** if "best for" labels lack workflow or buyer-fit justification
- **FAIL** if baseline-category pros are used as differentiators
- **FAIL** if conclusion is neutral equilibrium rather than compressed decision guidance
