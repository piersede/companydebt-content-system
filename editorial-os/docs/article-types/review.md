# Review Article Type — Authorship and Structure Rules

Extends the base rules in `docs/human-authorship-voice-engine.md` with review-specific requirements.

## Commercial review posture

- Vendor-authored critiques and reviews must declare their perspective early
- Methodology-first framing is preferred over verdict-first persuasion
- Critique must be specific, evidence-tagged, and more useful than simple vendor comparison copy
- The house product may not be used as a shortcut conclusion

## Review-specific authorship rules

- Every direct product-experience claim must contain at least one observed detail (Rule A)
- Every major strength must include its cost, condition, or setup burden (Rule E)
- Every major weakness must identify the exact operational consequence (Rule E)
- Each major section must return to the lived reality of the small-firm or team context (Rule C)
- Generic pros/cons phrasing is disallowed; if a pros/cons block is used, each bullet must read as a compressed observation, not a feature label (Rule G)
- The verdict must state the true problem the product solves, the true problem it does not solve, and the buyer fit / misfit clearly (Rule J)
- "Premium", "powerful", "flexible", "well-designed", "strong fit", and similar category labels must be interrogated and explained, not inherited (Rule I)

## Review pronoun defaults

Primary: we
Secondary: you
Tone: precise, tested, grounded
Human signal: visible proof of work, observed detail, real trade-offs

## Review-specific fail conditions

- **FAIL** if product-experience claims contain no observed detail
- **FAIL** if strengths or weaknesses lack friction (cost, condition, or consequence)
- **FAIL** if verdict restates rather than compresses the real decision
- **FAIL** if vendor/category labels are inherited without interrogation
