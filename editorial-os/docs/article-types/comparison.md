# Comparison Article Type — Authorship and Structure Rules

Extends the base rules in `docs/human-authorship-voice-engine.md` and `11-comparison-governance.md` with comparison-specific requirements.

## Comparison-specific authorship rules

- Do not inherit vendor framing or market shorthand without interrogation (Rule I)
- Do not reduce comparison logic to feature checklists without explaining workflow consequence
- Be the first to admit where the recommended option is weaker or not the right fit
- Use disarmament: credibility increases when the piece openly states where each tool is not the answer
- Avoid false symmetry and avoid false certainty
- Every recommendation must identify who should choose the tool and who should leave it
- Every comparative claim must be cashed out into operational impact, not just label difference (Rule B)

## Alternatives-page integrity

An alternatives page must not default to vendor-by-vendor summary mode. Before naming options, it must establish:

1. Why readers look for alternatives (the switching trigger)
2. What job they are actually trying to solve (the underlying problem)
3. Whether they need replacement, augmentation, or process change

If augmentation is a real buyer path, it must appear before the vendor list narrows the reader's frame.

## Operational explanation depth

The article must explain how the workflow works in practice, not merely describe features.

If the article distinguishes two tool categories, it must explain the operational difference directly.

Product sections must explain:
- how reminders or tasks are triggered
- what staff must do manually
- what happens when a client or user responds
- what automation actually changes

## SEO and decision utility

- Comparison pages require a richer matrix, not a thin summary table
- In-body contextual internal links are mandatory where relevant
- Key answers should exist in extractable short-answer formats
- The first fold must satisfy the searcher quickly without excessive UI clutter
- Add at least one expert-insight block that synthesises patterns rather than repeating product descriptions

## Comparison pronoun defaults

Primary: we
Secondary: you
Tone: precise, tested, grounded with clear editorial stance
Human signal: visible comparison methodology, acknowledged limitations, real trade-offs

## Comparison-specific fail conditions

- **FAIL** if vendor framing inherited without interrogation
- **FAIL** if comparison logic reduces to feature checklists without workflow consequence
- **FAIL** if no tool is identified as weaker or not-a-fit for any segment
- **FAIL** if false symmetry or false certainty is present
