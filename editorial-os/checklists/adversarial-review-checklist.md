# Adversarial Review Checklist (v1 — DEPRECATED)

> **DEPRECATED as of v2.1.** This checklist predates v2 governance and does not check against the 16 failure modes, AI prose fingerprints, Treatment 2 caps, or the two-part first-person test.
> Use instead: the inline adversarial review in `03-workflow-playbook.md` (Stage 7) and `06-prompt-library.md` (adversarial review prompt).
> Do not use this file as a compliance reference.

Attack the draft from three angles. If any section fails, flag it for rewrite.
## Sceptical expert angle
- [ ] Are there any claims a knowledgeable reader would challenge?
- [ ] Are there any generalisations stated as fact without supporting evidence?
- [ ] Are there any comparisons that unfairly favour one option?
- [ ] Is anything presented as novel that is actually common knowledge?
- [ ] Would an expert in this category trust this page?
## Time-poor buyer angle
- [ ] Can a busy reader find the main conclusion within 30 seconds?
- [ ] Is the BLUF (bottom line up front) strong and clear?
- [ ] Are sections scannable with useful subheads?
- [ ] Is there any section that adds length without adding decision value?
- [ ] Is the recommended action or next step obvious?
## Trust-sensitive reader angle
- [ ] Is it clear who wrote this and why their judgement matters?
- [ ] Are sources visible and verifiable?
- [ ] Are commercial relationships disclosed?
- [ ] Is there anything that reads like marketing copy rather than editorial assessment?
- [ ] Would a cautious reader feel comfortable acting on this advice?
## Generic content test
- [ ] Does every section add information gain beyond what a generic SaaS blog would provide?
- [ ] Are there any paragraphs that could appear on any competitor's site unchanged?
- [ ] Is there a clear editorial point of view, or does the piece read as neutral filler?
- [ ] Would removing any section make the piece weaker? (If not, remove it.)
## Decision usefulness test
- [ ] Does this piece help a specific reader make, narrow, or justify a specific decision?
- [ ] Are trade-offs visible and honestly framed?
- [ ] Is fit / non-fit guidance present and specific?
- [ ] Would the reader know what to do next after reading this?
## Pass / fail
- [ ] PASS — ready for human input insertion and final revision
- [ ] FAIL — flag sections for rewrite with specific notes
