# Comparison Page Outline — Company Debt Editorial OS

## 1. Bottom-line difference
[One paragraph: the core structural difference between the options. Not features — the design philosophy or trade-off that matters most.]

## 2. Best for X / best for Y
[Clear audience segmentation. Who should choose each option and why.]

## 3. Decision-trust module (required on vendor-perspective comparisons)
Fields:
- What this page can help you decide
- What it cannot decide for you
- Where publisher perspective may shape the analysis
- Which readers may reasonably land elsewhere

## 4. Perspective disclosure
Approved pattern: "This article was written by the Company Debt team and reflects our assessment of the options covered, based on [evidence basis]."
Must appear before substantive comparison begins.

## 5. Verification note
Fields:
- What was checked (product documentation, pricing pages, feature testing)
- When it was checked (month and year)
- What evidence basis was used

## 6. Scope note
Fields:
- What categories this page covers
- Whether the page includes replacement paths, adjacent tools, or both
- What the table does and does not claim to cover

## 7. Quick comparison table
[Side-by-side table covering the criteria that actually affect the decision. Label estimated or inferred data. Date the comparison.]

## 8. Differences that actually matter
[3-5 differences, each explained in terms of practical impact. Skip differences that do not change the decision.]

## 9. Pricing and contract comparison
[Like-for-like pricing where possible. Note where data is public vs estimated. Flag hidden costs.]

## 10. Feature and workflow comparison
[Grouped by what the reader is trying to do, not by product menus. Must explain workflow mechanics: how reminders are triggered, what staff do manually, what happens on client response.]

## 11. House-product-fit block (required where house product appears)
Fields:
- **Best fit:** who should use it and under what conditions
- **Not a fit:** who should not use it and why
- **Required conditions:** what must be true about the practice for adoption to make sense

## 12. Support and implementation comparison
[Setup time, onboarding, support responsiveness, migration path.]

## 13. Snippet-answer block
Purpose: short answer (40–60 words) to the primary search query this page targets. Definition or list format.

## 14. Which should you choose?
[Authored recommendation. Map specific situations to specific choices. Use guided scenario language (Rule K):
- "If you want X, choose Y"
- "If your primary problem is Z, start with W"
Preserve optionality: who should keep incumbent, who should switch, who should add a specialist layer.]

## 15. CTA block
[Must follow the editorial conclusion, not replace it. Must feel like the next step after the analysis.]

## 16. Methodology and evidence notes
[Sources reviewed. Verification method. Date of last check. Any commercial relationship disclosed.]
