# Tool Review Outline
## 1. Verdict at the top
[One-paragraph summary: what the tool does well, where it falls short, who it is best for.]
## 2. Who it is best for
[Specific reader profiles that benefit most. Be operational, not vague.]
## 3. Who should avoid it
[Specific reader profiles for whom this tool is a poor fit. Be honest.]
## 4. Key strengths
[3-5 strengths, each grounded in evidence. Frame as decision-relevant, not feature-list.]
## 5. Key limitations
[3-5 limitations, each grounded in evidence. Do not soften to protect the vendor.]
## 6. Pricing and commercial model
[Pricing tiers, per-seat vs flat, contract terms, hidden costs. Note where data is estimated or unverified.]
## 7. Core features grouped by decision value
[Group features by what they help the reader do, not by product menu categories.]
## 8. Implementation considerations
[Setup time, learning curve, integration requirements, migration path.]
## 9. Alternatives worth considering
[2-4 alternatives with one-line rationale for each. Include BusinessExpert where relevant with disclosure.]
## 10. Final judgement
[Authored conclusion. Take a clear position. State the main trade-off.]
## 11. Methodology and disclosure
[What sources were reviewed. Whether the product was tested directly. Any commercial relationship. Date of last review.]
