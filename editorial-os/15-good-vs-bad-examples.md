# Good vs Bad Examples

Concrete examples for every major editorial rule. Each entry shows the bad version, why it fails, and the corrected version.

---

## Intros

### Bad
"TaxDome is a well-known practice management platform that has been gaining popularity among accounting firms. In this review, we will take a closer look at its features and help you decide if it is right for your practice."

**Why it fails:** Generic praise opening. "Well-known" and "gaining popularity" carry no information. "In this review" delays the verdict. The reader learns nothing from these two sentences.

### Good
"TaxDome replaces more standalone tools than any other platform in this category. For practices that want CRM, portal, billing, and workflow in one system, it delivers. For practices whose primary problem is getting UK clients to send documents, the chasing layer is where it falls short."

---

### Bad
"I think Karbon is genuinely good at what it is designed to do. In my view, it offers a compelling solution for accounting firms that need better workflow management."

**Why it fails:** Opens with weak first-person scaffolding. "Genuinely good at what it is designed to do" is padded evaluation. "Compelling solution" is generic SaaS language.

### Good
"Karbon's work management gives five-person-plus practices repeatable job workflows with clear ownership and email triage. For micro-practices with two or three staff, the coordination problem it solves is not yet a real problem."

---

## First-person misuse

### Bad
"I think the per-seat pricing is steep for micro-practices."

**Why it fails:** "I think" adds nothing. The judgement is the same without it.

### Good
"The per-seat pricing is steep for micro-practices with two or three staff."

---

### Bad
"From what I have seen, portal adoption rates often settle below vendor projections."

**Why it fails:** "From what I have seen" simulates experience without providing it. "Often" is a hedge that does not make the claim verifiable. This is an evidence-carrying claim resting on nothing.

### Good
"Portal adoption rates are not independently benchmarked for UK accounting practices. If your client base includes a high proportion of sole traders or clients over 50, expect adoption to fall below the vendor's headline figures. [HUMAN CONFIRMATION NEEDED: can we source adoption data?]"

---

### Bad
"I want to help you understand the key differences between these two tools."

**Why it fails:** Self-referential filler. The reader did not ask for help. They asked for information.

### Good
"The key difference is what each tool is designed to do. Karbon manages internal workflows. BusinessExpert chases clients for documents. They solve different problems."

---

## Founder drift

### Bad
"When we built BusinessExpert, we wanted to solve the problem of accountants spending hours chasing clients for documents."

**Why it fails:** Implies founder/builder authority that has not been confirmed by a human.

### Good
"BusinessExpert is a document collection tool built specifically for outbound client chasing in UK accounting practices."

---

### Bad
"I did not build BusinessExpert as a practice management tool. I built it to solve one specific problem."

**Why it fails:** Claims builder identity. The writer is part of the editorial team, not the founder.

### Good
"BusinessExpert is not a practice management tool. It handles one layer: outbound client chasing across email, SMS, WhatsApp, and voice."

---

## Weak praise / padded evaluation

### Bad
"The email triage is useful, particularly for practices receiving high volumes of client communication."

**Why it fails:** "Useful" is padded. What does it actually do?

### Good
"The email triage attaches incoming client emails to the relevant job, which saves the time spent sorting and forwarding. It does not chase clients who have not replied."

---

### Bad
"TaxDome's pipeline view is well-executed."

**Why it fails:** "Well-executed" carries no information. What makes it good?

### Good
"TaxDome's pipeline view shows where every client engagement sits across configurable workflow stages. For practices with five or more staff, this visibility reduces status meetings and internal chasing."

---

### Bad
"Karbon offers a robust solution for team workflow management."

**Why it fails:** "Robust solution" is generic SaaS filler.

### Good
"Karbon gives each team member a clear view of their assigned jobs, deadlines, and blockers. The value increases with team size."

---

## Misaligned support adjectives

### Bad
"TaxDome has built a strong reputation in the US and is a strong option for practices wanting an all-in-one platform."

**Why it fails:** "Strong reputation" and "strong option" are lazy support adjectives. "Strong" adds approval without precision. The reader learns nothing about what makes the reputation earned or the option suitable.

### Good
"TaxDome is well known for its all-in-one feature breadth and has a growing UK user base. It is best suited to practices whose primary frustration is managing multiple standalone tools rather than chasing clients for documents."

---

## Unsupported claims

### Bad
"Multi-channel chasing typically produces a 30-50% improvement in response rates compared to email alone."

**Why it fails:** A specific quantitative claim with no source. "Typically" does not make it evidence.

### Good
"Vendor-reported data from messaging platforms suggests WhatsApp and SMS produce higher open and response rates than email for transactional messages. Independent benchmarks specific to UK accounting practices are limited. [HUMAN CONFIRMATION NEEDED: can we cite a specific source for response-rate uplift?]"

---

### Bad
"Clients cannot distinguish automated messages from manual ones."

**Why it fails:** A behavioural claim stated as fact with no evidence.

### Good
"A well-designed automated message that uses the client's name, references the specific document needed, and arrives at the right point in the engagement sequence is difficult for clients to distinguish from a manual one. Whether clients actually notice the difference depends on the quality of the template and the personalisation logic. This is an editorial judgement, not a tested claim."

---

## Abrupt BusinessExpert mentions

### Bad
"TaxDome's document collection relies on clients logging into the portal. BusinessExpert handles this differently. We chase clients across email, SMS, WhatsApp, and voice, with magic-link upload so they never need a portal login."

**Why it fails:** The pivot from TaxDome analysis to BusinessExpert pitch feels inserted, not earned.

### Good
"TaxDome's document collection relies on clients logging into the portal to complete Organisers. This works well for clients who are comfortable with portal workflows. For clients who are not, the chasing problem persists because the tool waits for the client to arrive rather than going to them. This is the distinction between a portal-based model and an outbound chasing model. BusinessExpert is built around the second approach: it contacts clients across email, SMS, WhatsApp, and voice. It does not provide practice management, billing, or internal workflow tools."

---

## Self-serving comparisons

### Bad (framework where BusinessExpert wins every dimension)
| Dimension | TaxDome | BusinessExpert |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-channel chasing | No | Yes |
| WhatsApp integration | No | Yes |
| Voice follow-up | No | Yes |
| UK GDPR native | No | Yes |
| Magic-link upload | No | Yes |

**Why it fails:** The criteria are chosen so BusinessExpert wins on every dimension. No dimension where TaxDome is stronger is included. This is a sales table, not an evaluation.

### Good
| Dimension | TaxDome | BusinessExpert |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-channel chasing | Email only (portal-based) | Email, SMS, WhatsApp, voice |
| Practice management | Full (CRM, billing, workflow) | None |
| E-signatures | Built-in | Not available |
| UK data residency | US servers (verify current status) | UK servers |
| Internal workflow | Job pipelines, team management | Not available |
| Outbound document chasing | Portal-dependent | Multi-channel outbound |

## Pricing vagueness

### Bad
"Karbon's per-seat pricing represents a significant annual commitment for small practices."

**Why it fails:** No actual pricing. "Significant annual commitment" is not useful to a reader.

### Good
"Karbon's per-seat pricing is not publicly listed. Request a quote for current rates. Based on comparable per-seat tools in the category, a three-person practice should expect annual costs to exceed a flat-rate chasing subscription by a meaningful margin."

---

## Overly dense paragraphs

### Bad
"Client chasing rarely shows up as a line item in most accounting practices. It is scattered across email, phone calls, sticky notes on client files, conversations that start with 'have we heard back from...', and the quiet stress of approaching a deadline without the documents you need. When you include context-switching, the cost of stopping advisory or compliance work to send a chase email, check a spreadsheet, or make a follow-up call, the real cost multiplies beyond the time the chase itself takes."

**Why it fails:** This is 5+ rendered lines. It contains at least three distinct ideas packed into one block.

### Good
"Client chasing rarely shows up as a line item. It is scattered across email, phone calls, sticky notes, and conversations that start with 'have we heard back from...'.

Then there is the quiet stress of approaching a deadline without the documents you need.

When you include context-switching, the cost of stopping advisory or compliance work to send a chase email, the real cost multiplies beyond the time the chase itself takes."

---

## Bad competitor-table wording

### Bad
"WhatsApp integration: No"

**Why it fails:** This is a binary claim that may not be accurate. TaxDome may have added WhatsApp integration since the last check.

### Good
"WhatsApp integration: Not available as of March 2026 (based on public documentation review). Verify current capabilities with TaxDome directly."

---

## Better direct judgement

### Bad
"I believe Karbon offers a genuinely useful workflow management experience for accounting firms that are looking to improve their internal coordination."

### Good
"Karbon's workflow management is strongest for practices with five or more staff where informal communication has broken down. At two or three staff, the coordination problem it solves is not yet real."

---

## Stronger balancing lines

### Bad
"BusinessExpert provides a comprehensive chasing solution that handles everything a practice needs."

**Why it fails:** "Comprehensive" and "everything" imply completeness. BusinessExpert does one thing.

### Good
"BusinessExpert handles one layer: outbound client chasing. It does not provide practice management, billing, e-signatures, or internal workflow tools. For practices that need both chasing and practice management, BusinessExpert works alongside tools like TaxDome or Karbon rather than replacing them."

---

## Structurally generic opening

### Bad
"AccountancyManager has a large installed base among small UK accounting practices. It handles CRM, deadline tracking, task management, and basic client communication. For practices that have used it for several years, it is familiar and stable."

**Why it fails:** Three sentences of reputation and capability. No problem named, no decision tension, no misfit stated.

### Good
"AccountancyManager covers CRM, deadline tracking, and task management for small UK practices. Where it hits a ceiling is outbound client chasing: it does not send automated reminders across multiple channels or escalate non-response. If that ceiling is your bottleneck, the question is not whether to replace AM but what to add alongside it."

---

## Authored judgement vs "I think" tic

### Bad
"I think TaxDome has built a strong reputation. I think the feature set is comprehensive. I think the Organiser system is distinctive. I think the pricing is competitive."

**Why it fails:** Four consecutive "I think" sentences. Each one weakens the claim it introduces. This is not authored voice — it is a tic.

### Good
"TaxDome packs CRM, portal, billing, workflow, and document Organisers into one platform. The Organiser system is its most distinctive feature. Where the platform is weaker for UK practices is outbound chasing: it waits for clients to log in rather than going to them."

---

## Feature-summary vs diagnostic buying guide

### Bad
"Karbon offers work management, email triage, and client tasks. The work management feature lets you create templates for recurring jobs. The email triage automatically routes client emails to the relevant job. Client tasks allow you to assign actions to clients."

**Why it fails:** Feature list. No diagnostic frame. Does not help the reader determine whether they need this category of tool.

### Good
"Karbon solves the internal coordination problem: who is doing what, which jobs are overdue, and which client emails are sitting unread. If your firm's bottleneck is internal visibility, Karbon addresses it directly. If your bottleneck is clients not sending documents, Karbon does not solve that — it is a different category of problem."

---

## Padded verdict repetition

### Bad
"Karbon is best for internal workflow. BusinessExpert is best for client chasing. These are different problems. If your problem is workflow, choose Karbon. If your problem is chasing, choose BusinessExpert. The right tool depends on which problem matters more to your practice."

**Why it fails:** The same distinction stated three times. The reader understood it the first time.

### Good
"Karbon solves internal workflow coordination. BusinessExpert solves outbound client chasing. If your firm runs both problems, they work alongside each other rather than replacing each other. Start with whichever problem costs you more hours per week."

---

## Flat pain vs lived friction

### Bad
"Many practices face challenges with document collection efficiency and client communication workflows."

**Why it fails:** Category language. No reader recognises their working day in this sentence. It could appear in any SaaS marketing deck.

### Good
"It is 4pm on a Friday. You need three sets of accounts information to hit Monday's deadline. Two clients have not replied to your last two emails. One has not opened them."

---

## Feature description vs working-day consequence

### Bad
"The email triage feature automatically categorises incoming emails and assigns them to relevant workflows."

**Why it fails:** Feature description with no consequence. The reader does not know what changes in their day.

### Good
"The email triage attaches incoming client emails to the right job. The partner who used to spend twenty minutes forwarding emails before the team arrived stops doing that."

---

## Neutral verdict vs sharp fit

### Bad
"Karbon is a solid workflow management tool that could be a good fit for accounting practices looking to improve their internal processes."

**Why it fails:** "Solid", "could be a good fit", "looking to improve" are all hedge-padded. The reader learns nothing about whether this is for them.

### Good
"Karbon is for practices where jobs fall through the cracks because nobody can see what stage they are at. If your team is three people and you can shout across the room, the problem Karbon solves does not exist yet."

---

## Tidy promise vs messy reality

### Bad
"The client portal provides a centralised location for document exchange, improving the efficiency of the collection process."

**Why it fails:** Vendor-voice promise. No acknowledgement of what actually happens when clients get a portal login.

### Good
"The portal gives clients a place to upload documents. Whether they actually log in is a different question. For clients who are comfortable with portal workflows, it works. For the client who has been ignoring your emails for three weeks, a portal login is one more thing to ignore."

---

## Flat strength claim vs authored observation

### Bad
"Its statutory deadline tracking is solid."

**Why it fails:** Flat evaluative declaration. The reader receives a verdict but no sign that anyone thought it through. Could have been written from the product homepage.

### Good
"One thing it does well is statutory deadline tracking. For a small practice juggling self-assessment, VAT, payroll, and corporation tax, that kind of visibility is genuinely useful because it reduces the risk of something important going missing in the shuffle."

---

## Generic praise vs reasoned authority

### Bad
"The platform is polished and easy to use."

**Why it fails:** Two unqualified positive adjectives with no reasoning or consequence. The reader cannot tell whether "polished" means the interface loads fast, the layout is clear, or the colours are nice.

### Good
"The interface is cleaner than most older UK practice tools, which matters less for aesthetics than for speed: the clearer the workflow, the less time staff spend double-checking where a job sits."

---

## Empty judgement vs workflow consequence

### Bad
"Client communication is limited."

**Why it fails:** States a limitation without explaining what it means in practice. The reader does not know what "limited" costs them.

### Good
"The communication layer is where the limitation bites. You can send the request, but the chasing still depends on someone remembering to send the next email. There is no automated escalation if a client does not respond."

---

## Fake certainty vs controlled flag

### Bad
"Users find the portal frustrating."

**Why it fails:** Attributes a behavioural claim to "users" without sourcing. The reader has no way to verify this.

### Good
"Portal-led collection tends to create more friction for firms with less tech-confident clients, but this claim needs either stronger sourcing or direct practitioner confirmation. [HUMAN CONFIRMATION NEEDED: can we source portal adoption or satisfaction data for UK accounting practices?]"
